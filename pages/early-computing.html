Early Computing

From Ancient Tools to Mechanical Computers

The Abacus (3000 BC)

The first known calculating tool, used in ancient civilizations. This simple yet effective device allowed merchants and mathematicians to perform calculations quickly and accurately.

Antikythera Mechanism (100-150 BC)

An ancient Greek analog computer used to predict astronomical positions and eclipses. This sophisticated device was centuries ahead of its time.

Slide Rule (1620s)

William Oughtred invented the slide rule, which became the most commonly used calculation tool in science and engineering until the 1970s.

Pascal's Calculator (1642)

Blaise Pascal invented the mechanical calculator capable of performing additions and subtractions automatically.

Jacquard Loom (1804)

First use of punched cards to control a machine, introducing the concept of programmable devices.

Analytical Engine (1837)

Charles Babbage's design for a mechanical computer that could be programmed using punched cards. Ada Lovelace wrote the first algorithm intended to be processed by this machine.
