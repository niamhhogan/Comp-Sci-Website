History of Computing Timeline

Abacus

c. 2400 BC

Ancient Babylonian counting tool, earliest known calculating device.

Slide Rule

1620

Invented by William Oughtred, revolutionized mathematical calculations.

Mechanical Calculators

1642

Pascal's calculator (Pascaline) performs automatic calculations.

Difference Engine

1822

Babbage's automatic mechanical calculator for polynomial functions.

Analytical Engine

1837

First general-purpose computer design with memory and programs.

Boolean Algebra

1847

George Boole develops mathematical basis for digital computing.

Vacuum Tubes

1904

First electronic computing component, enabling digital circuits.

Turing Machine

1936

Alan Turing defines theoretical basis for computer programming.

Z3 Computer

1941

First programmable, fully automatic computing machine by Konrad Zuse.

Colossus

1943

First electronic digital computer, used to break Nazi codes.

ENIAC

1945

First general-purpose electronic computer, capable of 5000 operations per second.

Transistor

1947

Invention at Bell Labs revolutionizes electronics, replacing vacuum tubes.

COBOL

1959

First widely-used business programming language, developed by Grace Hopper.

Integrated Circuit

1959

Multiple transistors on a single chip, enabling smaller computers.

Apollo Guidance Computer

1966

First embedded computer system, helped land humans on the Moon.

ARPANET

1969

First computer network, predecessor to the modern Internet.

Microprocessor

1971

Intel 4004: First commercial microprocessor on a single chip.

Personal Computer

1981

IBM PC standardizes personal computing, launching a revolution.

World Wide Web

1989

Tim Berners-Lee invents the Web at CERN, revolutionizing information sharing.

Smartphones

2007

iPhone launches, making mobile computing accessible to everyone.

AI Revolution

2022-Present

Large Language Models and AI transform computing and society.
